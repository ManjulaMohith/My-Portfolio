Have you ever wondered how aliens and certain characters in TV and films get their unique looks? Of course, it’s a team effort on the part of the writers and directors, but the artist that makes that vision reality is a Special effects makeup artist

Most productions have a makeup person in charge of keeping everyone looking sharp on camera. But when special effects like bruises, cuts, fake blood, or lacerations are required, the SFX makeup artist is called. For shows with aliens or unique makeup, these artists get the pleasure of creating some of the most memorable parts of the series.

What is Special Effects Makeup?

Special effects makeup, or SFX makeup, is the use of cosmetics and prosthetics to create special effects for camera, film, or live-action theater. The effects can be simple, like bruises or fake blood from a fistfight. Or they can be complex and elaborate, like brain-eating zombies or aliens from other planets. The only limit is your creativity.
As a career, SFXmakeup artists range from part-time hobbyists who work with local theater troupes or film schools to full-time salaried professionals working on major film projects.
 

Special FX Makeup Schools

Since it is a creative field, SFX makeup artists are always online sharing their latest creations and techniques. There are countless video tutorials and numerous blogs with a wealth of information. They range in experience level from novices to salaried professionals. Many use drug-store cosmetics while others use a professional special effects makeup kit.

The great thing about using online tutorials and practicing at home on friends is that you can get a feel for what it takes to get into SFX makeup without committing. If you have a knack and passion for it, then you can pursue going to one of the special effectsmakeup schools while you continue to experiment with your new hobby.

What Goes in an SFX Makeup Kit?

  • Applicators, including brushes, swabs, powder puffs, and sponges. It’s good to have a variety of sponges, as coarser sponges can be used to create amazing textures.
  • A pallette for mixing makeup and modeling compounds.
  • A “bruise and abrasions wheel” that contains makeup of several colors suited to creating bruise effects. They typically include purples, browns, reds, and yellows.
  • Liquid latex that can be used to create realistic-looking cuts, burns, and lacerations. It is also sometimes used as an adhesive for prosthetic makeup.
  • Castor seal is handy to have around when using liquid latex. When you use it on top of liquid latex, it allows makeup to be applied more easily and prevents discoloring.
  • A 3D modeling compound for sculpting 3D skin effects.
  • Water-soluble glue or spirit gum and remover. These adhesives are used for sticking things to the performer’s face including hair, fake noses, and bald caps.
  • Fake blood (of course!).
